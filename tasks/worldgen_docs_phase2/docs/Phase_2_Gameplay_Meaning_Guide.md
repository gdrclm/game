# Phase_2_Gameplay_Meaning_Guide
## Design-facing gameplay meaning guide for Phase 2 outputs
**Repository:** `gdrclm/game`  
**Status:** Draft design guide after completed Phase 1  
**Audience:** design, engineering, Codex implementation passes  
**Scope:** explains what Phase 2 outputs should mean in gameplay terms without replacing later bridge layers

---

# 1. Purpose

This guide exists to prevent a common failure mode:

> Phase 2 generates many fields, but nobody can clearly say what kind of player experience they imply.

This file translates Phase 2 outputs into **gameplay meaning language**.

---

# 2. Core rule

Phase 2 fields do not directly equal gameplay values.  
They imply **planning conditions**.

Phase 2 should answer:
- what kind of run this island/region implies;
- what the player is pressured to plan around;
- what the player may still rely on;
- where the decision-space is narrow or wide.

---

# 3. Pressure meaning

## High survivability pressure means:
- staying alive is intrinsically costly;
- recovery loops are fragile;
- raw endurance matters more.

## High mobility pressure means:
- movement itself is a problem;
- route choice matters;
- travel cost dominates.

## High supply pressure means:
- access to basic support is unreliable;
- risk of attrition rises;
- fallback options matter more.

## High chokepoint stress means:
- few route decisions matter disproportionately;
- forced passage logic becomes more important;
- detours become expensive.

## High remoteness burden means:
- mistakes are harder to recover from;
- support delay matters;
- peripheral isolation changes planning.

## High ecological burden means:
- stable support zones are fragile;
- recovery is harder to trust;
- local resource assumptions weaken.

## High catastrophe susceptibility means:
- major break events matter;
- risk concentration is severe;
- planning must account for disruption.

---

# 4. Rhythm meaning

## High seasonality strength means:
- the world changes in readable cycles;
- timing matters.

## High storm cadence means:
- disruptive weather-like phases recur often enough to matter.

## High navigation rhythm volatility means:
- route viability is timing-sensitive.

## High scarcity cadence means:
- support fluctuates through cycles rather than staying flat.

## High predictability means:
- timing rewards planning.

## High rupture frequency means:
- expected rhythm breaks often.

## Strong recovery profile means:
- the world gives real recovery windows;
- pressure is not constant punishment.

---

# 5. Recovery / relief meaning

Recovery and relief are not decoration.

They mean:
- when the world loosens its grip;
- whether risk is continuous or punctuated;
- whether planning can buy safety;
- whether endurance-only play is the only viable style.

If recovery is weak everywhere, gameplay narrows.  
If recovery is strong everywhere, burden loses identity.  
Therefore recovery must create **meaningful contrast**, not universal softness.

---

# 6. Planning-style meanings

Different Phase 2 profiles should imply different planning styles.

Examples:

## Endurance planning
Dominant when:
- burden is high
- rhythm is readable
- recovery is weak but stable

## Timing-window planning
Dominant when:
- navigation rhythm matters
- relief windows exist
- storm/rupture timing matters

## Route-risk planning
Dominant when:
- mobility pressure is high
- chokepoint stress is high
- route fragility is high

## Scarcity-conservation planning
Dominant when:
- supply pressure is high
- scarcity cadence is strong
- recovery is limited

## Shelter-dependency planning
Dominant when:
- exposure is high
- shelter reliability matters
- calm intervals are narrow

---

# 7. Anti-misread rules

Do not read:
- high pressure = good difficulty by itself
- high rhythm volatility = automatically exciting
- low predictability = automatically better
- high catastrophe susceptibility = enough on its own
- recovery = softness

The goal is not more suffering.  
The goal is more **meaningful planning texture**.

---

# 8. Design questions every profile should answer

For any strong Phase 2 output, design should be able to answer:

1. What does the player fear here?
2. What does the player still trust here?
3. What kind of planning is rewarded here?
4. What kind of mistake is punished here?
5. Does this world give relief, and if yes, how?

If those answers are vague, the profile is not gameplay-readable enough.

---

# 9. Final statement

This guide exists so Phase 2 stays connected to player experience.

A good Phase 2 output is not just “harsh”.  
It implies a **specific way to play, plan, and survive**.
