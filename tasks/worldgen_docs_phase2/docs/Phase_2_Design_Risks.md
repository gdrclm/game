# Phase_2_Design_Risks
## Official design-risk register for Phase 2
**Repository:** `gdrclm/game`  
**Status:** Draft design-risk document after completed Phase 1  
**Audience:** design, engineering, Codex implementation passes  
**Scope:** identifies the main game-design failure modes of Phase 2

---

# 1. Purpose

This document exists so Phase 2 does not become architecturally correct but game-design weak.

---

# 2. Major risks

## Risk A — Scalar difficulty collapse
Phase 2 silently degrades into one big harshness score.

## Risk B — Punishment-only world
The world pressures the player constantly but gives no meaningful relief.

## Risk C — Pressure/rhythm collapse
Timing becomes just another burden multiplier.

## Risk D — Recovery loss
Recovery fields exist on paper but are too weak or too detached to matter.

## Risk E — Climate duplication
Phase 2 wastes effort rebuilding climate instead of interpreting completed Phase 1 climate truth.

## Risk F — Handoff semantic leakage
Political/history-facing handoff hints leak into Phase 2 and contaminate pre-interpretive environmental truth.

## Risk G — Record blindness
Completed Phase 1 records exist, but Phase 2 stays anonymous field math.

## Risk H — Gameplay detachment
Phase 2 becomes deep simulation with no meaningful path into progression, layout, spawn, or map layers.

## Risk I — Identical planning style
Different regions differ numerically but produce the same player behavior.

## Risk J — Decorative summaries
Summaries sound smart but do not correspond to actual field logic.

---

# 3. Design response principle

For every Phase 2 implementation pass, design should ask:
- does this increase planning texture?
- does this preserve relief?
- does this preserve difference between burden and timing?
- does this remain environmental rather than political?
- does this stay connected to the real game?

---

# 4. Final statement

This file exists to keep Phase 2 from becoming:
- more complicated,
- but less useful.
