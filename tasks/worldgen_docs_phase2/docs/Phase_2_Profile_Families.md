# Phase_2_Profile_Families
## Official environmental profile families for Phase 2
**Repository:** `gdrclm/game`  
**Status:** Draft design-facing profile-family document after completed Phase 1  
**Audience:** design, engineering, Codex implementation passes  
**Scope:** defines readable environmental profile families that Phase 2 outputs should be able to express

---

# 1. Purpose

This document exists so Phase 2 can be discussed in readable profile families instead of raw field dumps only.

These are not narrative themes.  
They are environmental planning families.

---

# 2. Core rule

A profile family is valid only if it is:
- field-backed
- gameplay-readable
- environmental, not political
- usable in validation and debugging

---

# 3. Core profile families

## 3.1 Harsh but predictable
- high burden
- high predictability
- low rupture
- moderate or weak recovery

Implies:
- endurance planning
- reliable caution

## 3.2 Route volatile
- high travel exposure
- unstable navigation windows
- high chokepoint stress
- moderate burden outside route layer

Implies:
- route-risk planning
- timing around movement

## 3.3 Scarcity cyclic
- moderate/high supply pressure
- strong scarcity cadence
- recovery windows exist but are narrow

Implies:
- conservation planning
- timing around support cycles

## 3.4 Low relief high burden
- high burden
- weak recovery
- weak forgiveness
- low stable-zone likelihood

Implies:
- oppressive runs
- very narrow recovery planning space

## 3.5 Calm until rupture
- moderate baseline burden
- moderate/high predictability
- low-to-moderate cadence noise
- high rupture significance

Implies:
- planning that is rewarded until major breaks occur

## 3.6 Isolation dominant
- high remoteness burden
- weak support delay tolerance
- moderate/high route fragility
- recovery depends on rare safe structures

Implies:
- remote-play caution
- mistake minimization

## 3.7 Recovery-rich but unstable
- moderate burden
- meaningful recovery windows
- weak predictability
- timing-sensitive relief

Implies:
- opportunistic planning
- fast adaptation play

---

# 4. Contradiction families

These are especially valuable for design.

## 4.1 Harsh but recoverable
High burden, but good recovery windows.

## 4.2 Low burden but unstable timing
World is not intrinsically harsh, but timing is unreliable.

## 4.3 Scarce but readable
Support is weak, but cycles are trustworthy.

## 4.4 Dangerous routes, stable shelter
Travel is harsh, but refuge logic is reliable.

These combinations often generate stronger gameplay identity than one-note hardship.
